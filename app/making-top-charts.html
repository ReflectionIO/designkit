More Money, Fewer Downloads

Armed with the average download or revenue numbers required to hit a certain chart position in a specific country, you can better plan:

  • What type of app to make
  • Where in the world to release
  • What app store category or sub-category to release an app in

For instance, the 'Casino' game subcategory generates higher revenue from fewer downloads than many others (see left)

It's important to note when planning monetisation strategies that the iOS Top Grossing chart is comprised of paid download and in-app purchase revenue only, advertising revenue is not included.

Ease into the Paid Charts

It takes a remarkably small number of daily downloads to break into the top 200 Paid app chart in almost all categories. Some are particularly astonishing; 'Weather' and 'News' for example require only a single download per day in the USA on average (the largest market). This falls to mere fractions of a download (based on the average) for smaller markets like the UK.

How do Countries Compare?

Data compiled from the USA and major European countries (see left) reveals some interesting trends when compared per capita. Whilst the USA clearly dominates the sample group in market size and spending power, revenue generated by ‘Productivity’ apps spikes in Germany, where it is nearly double that of the UK.

German users also turn out to have the deepest pockets on Navigation apps, out-spending even the Americans. The theme continues into Weather apps where again the Germans are free spending; double the average of the UK or France, Europe’s other large markets.

How to Move Through the Ranks

It's invaluable to know the gap between ranking at say position 200 and position 10 for your chosen category in a specific country (e.g Games, USA). With this information it’s possible to plan exactly what marketing is necessary to acquire enough downloads or revenue to climb to the desired level.

Smaller gaps between adjacent bars on the right indicate a category or country in which it may be easier to climb to a higher rank with fewer downloads and thus less marketing effort.
