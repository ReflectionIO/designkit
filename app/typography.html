Typography

Below are the styling rules for sitewide typography. Our brand typefaces are clean, uncomplicated and easy to read at any size. Specific spacing rules to ensure legibility and UX integrity are detailed below alongside examples.

Typefaces

Our brand fonts reflect our core values of simplicity and clarity. They not only look beautifully uncluttered but are also functional, being legible at any size.

Opens Sans is our title font, used for all headings, labels, buttons and calls to action.

Lato is used as body copy in all blocks of text, table data and additionally in tabs and navigation menus where all caps links are required.

Typography Styles

Headings

Headings are all Opens Sans Semibold with a hex ref colour of #29292c. The bottom margins, font sizes and line height associated with each type of heading is shown on the right.

Typically H1 titles are used for main promo or hero area titles of fewer words. H2 and H3 are used more frequently as section or sub section headings and work better with longer titles.

Body Copy

Body copy is all Lato Regular with a colour of #5b5b68. There are 3 main sizes based on application and size of container (see right for sizes and line-heights).

Feature body copy is used sparingly in short bursts as an introduction or to draw attention to important content.

Main body copy is the most widely used and should be the default when creating text content. However if the containing element is small or narrow or the content is less important then Minor body copy must be used to maintain legibility and content hierarchy.

Bolded text is Lato Bold and coloured #29292c for extra prominence.

Link text in Lato Bold, coloured #6d69c5 and has the rollover effect seen on the right.

Lists

Unordered lists are Lato Regular and #5b5b68. There are 3 sizes based on the prominence required for the list items: Large, Mid and Small each with differeing font sizes and lin-heights (see right).

Ordered lists use exactly the same styles but instead of a dash icon have a number which is Open Sans Regular in #c4c7d0 at the Large size but then Open Sans Semibold for the Mid and Small versions (see right).
